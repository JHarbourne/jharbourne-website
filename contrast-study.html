Academic Research Study

Colour Contrast Perception

Can you tell us which text is easier to read?

Current web accessibility standards (WCAG) state that text must achieve a 4.5:1 contrast ratio – a single fixed number applied to every colour. But does your eye actually work that way?

This study investigates whether the colour (hue) of a background affects the perceived readability of text – independently of the standard luminance-based ratio. Your responses will help us understand whether the current standard captures real human perception across the full colour spectrum.

The study takes approximately 8–12 minutes. You will be shown pairs of text samples and asked to click the one that looks easier to read – like an optician’s chart, but for colour contrast.

What is collected – and what isn’t

This study collects your responses, response times, and some brief questions about your vision and display conditions. It does not collect your name, email address, or IP address. Your study data never leaves your own browser – there is no server upload unless you choose to submit it. Raw data files will be deleted once statistical analysis is complete; only anonymised aggregate findings will be published.
